    An NLP Case Study: Efficient, Billion-Scale Text Classification

    Natural Language Processing (NLP) forms the backbone of AI-driven automated response systems. In these systems, understanding the sentiment and intent of a user is a critical component. As an example, the sentence “I love milk but it makes me lethargic” is something humans understand as having negative sentiment. It is, however, non-trivial for computers to figure this out. Most commercial recommendation, search, and service systems rely heavily on identifying these subtle text nuances to streamline offerings.

    Deep Learning and recent advances in NLP have made it possible to understand the intent of a query through massive, carefully engineered models trained on equally massive, carefully curated datasets. However, these models rely on a fleet of expensive GPUs to offer acceptable real-time latency.

    The ThirdAI Difference

    ThirdAI delivers high accuracy and performance AI on commodity CPUs. Our technology is based on over 10 years of research in hash-based processing algorithms, allowing us to not only train models much faster on CPUs than GPUs, but also handle much larger models in general. As a result, our commodity CPUs are well positioned to capitalize on the scaling laws of neural networks.

    Sentiment Classification on Yelp Reviews

    To test text classification we trained a neural network using ThirdAI’s BOLT Engine on a Yelp review sentiment dataset. The top alternative using GPUs for this task is the popular RoBERTa model. The following table showcases BOLT’s superiority in all domains, including accuracy, training time, latency, and cost.

    Method Accuracy Training time Inference latency (per query on CPU) No. of parameters (millions) Annual cost: 100M queries/week** Annual cost: Fine-tuning once per week**
    RoBERTa (fine tuned for sentiment) 83.02% Several days pre-training on GPUs with 40hrs fine-tuning. 46 ms 355M $400k (USD) $52k (USD)
    ThirdAI’s BOLT Engine 92.76% 2 hours trained from scratch on a commodity CPU. 1 ms 2000M (2B) $2.3k (USD) Trivial

    ** Cost to use RoBERTa assumes p3.16x large instances on AWS. BOLT uses a single r6i.x large CPU instance on AWS. RoBERTa inference on CPU is 46 ms. BOLT inference on CPU is 1 ms. RoBERTA requires a GPU box to reach 10 ms per query.

    By using BOLT to train on CPUs, we receive substantial gains on all relevant metrics. Firstly, we note a ~10% improvement in accuracy due to the sheer size of model we are able to train, a feat infeasible on GPUs due to memory constraints. Secondly, we note greatly improved training and inference times on commodity CPUs, drastically reducing end-to-end deployment costs.
